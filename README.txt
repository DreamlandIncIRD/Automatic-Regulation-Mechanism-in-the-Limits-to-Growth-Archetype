## Abstract
This paper examines the automatic regulation mechanism within the "Limits to Growth" archetype, using the taxi market as an example. Four scenarios are considered: constant demand, step-by-step demand changes, the introduction of a driver entry delay time, and periodic demand changes. 
It is shown that the equilibrium number of taxis is determined not by technical capacity, but by the number of passengers. The system automatically reaches equilibrium through a Job attractiveness mechanism defined as the ratio of actual income to target income. 
Changes in demand shift the equilibrium, while a delay in decision‑making reduces the number of taxis and increases income. Periodic demand changes do not destabilize the system.
